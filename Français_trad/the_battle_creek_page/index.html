Country of Origin : Ethiopia

The Battle Creek's coffee plantation had been the first one to be discovered by Columbo Robusto during his several trips around the world. He first found the Ethiopian one when he traveled across the whole Africa ; from Algeria to Madagascar, in 1979...

... Legend has it that Mr. Robusto arrived in a tiny village in southern Ethiopia, and was welcomed by a mother with her child, who gave him one white flower each as a goodwill token. He then found where this flower come from, a coffee plantation !

About this product :

Battle Creek's beans have quite a rough shell, that's why we pre-split them just before packing them. They are farmed, harvested, packed and sent in the most ecological way possible...

... And that's why they have the Ecocert label since 2001, and won the World Coffee Challenge of 2021. They're also produced in Peru, in similar conditions, in a coffee farm next to Cuzco.

More detailed description :

Ethiopia and Peru :

Coffee culture is important in Ethiopia and Peru, seen as a symbol of hospitality and generosity. Ethiopia's inhabitants give a great attention to coffee quality and preparation because it's strongly linked to their traditions and ceremonies. In fact, Ethiopia is the birthplace of Arabica.

Bean species :

Kontir (Arabica)

Kontir variety grows in a wild, mountainous, forest region in Djimmah, southern Ethiopia. Kontir is known for its unique aroma, sweet taste and positive impact on environment and local communities, due to its crop growing made in small batches by local producers.
