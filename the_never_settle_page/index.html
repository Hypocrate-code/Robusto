More detailed description :

Viêt Nam :

Introduced by the French in the late 19th century, coffee production in Vietnam faced challenges during the wars, but saw a resurgence in the 1990s, due to the quality of Vietnamese lands. Today, Vietnam is the second-largest coffee producer in the world, known for its robusta beans and innovative processing techniques.

Bean species :

Robusta

Robusta is a type of coffee bean with a strong and distinct flavor that is less desirable than Arabica beans, but prized for its high caffeine content. It is often used in blends to create a balanced flavor profile with a richer taste. When roasted and brewed correctly, Robusta coffee can be a delicious and enjoyable drink.

Going from light yellow to dark brown, the color of the bean can change due to roasting level and personal preferences. A darker coffee will have a stronger, smokier flavor and lower acidity and cafeine, whereas a lighter coffee will have a more delicate flavor and higher acidity and cafeine.
